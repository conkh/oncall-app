**INPUT DATA:**
- Workers: [LIST_OF_WORKERS]
- Weeks to Schedule: [NUMBER_OF_WEEKS] or [LIST_OF_WEEKS]
- Approved Time-Offs: [LIST_OF_APPROVED_TIME_OFFS]
- Pre-requested Assignments: [LIST_OF_PRE_ASSIGNMENTS]

**DEFINITIONS:**
- Each week requires exactly one "First On-Call" and exactly one "Second On-Call".
- Total On-Call = The sum of a worker's First On-Call and Second On-Call shifts.

**RULES & CONSTRAINTS:**
- Constraint 1: A worker CANNOT be assigned the "First On-Call" role for 2 consecutive weeks.
- Constraint 2: A worker CANNOT be assigned ANY on-call role (First or Second) for 3 consecutive weeks.

**EXECUTION STEPS:**
You must process the schedule strictly in the following order:

**Step 1: Process Time-Off and Pre-requested Assignments (Priority Override)**
- Block out all workers who have approved time-off during specific weeks. They cannot be scheduled for those weeks.
- Place all "Pre-requested Assignments" into the schedule first. 
- *Note: Pre-requested assignments and take-off are absolute priorities and bypass Constraints 1 and 2 if necessary.*

**Step 2: Balance "First On-Call"**
- Fill in the remaining "First On-Call" slots for the entire calendar.
- Distribute these shifts as evenly as possible among all available workers to ensure fairness.
- You MUST strictly enforce Constraint 1 and Constraint 2 during this step.

**Step 3: Balance Total On-Call**
- Fill in the remaining "Second On-Call" slots for the entire calendar.
- Balance the overall on-call workload (First + Second) as evenly as possible across all workers.
- You MUST strictly enforce Constraint 2 during this step. Ensure no worker is scheduled for any combination of roles for 3 weeks in a row.
